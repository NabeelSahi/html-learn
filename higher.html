Higher Education Committees

CIS committee members play an important role in the delivery of student recruitment events. The Committees have been established to leverage the expertise of individuals from member institutions who are experienced leaders in university admission and recruitment.The CIS Higher Education Committee serves as an advisory board for CIS and is comprised of representatives from CIS Regional Service Committees, the CIS Guidance Committee, as well as appointed and ex-officio members. The planning and leading of Student Recruitment Tours is conducted by the CIS Regional Service Committees serving each world region. The CIS Guidance Committee is a volunteer committee which supports the work of the CIS Regional Service Committees, plans and leads the annual CIS University Counsellor Tour and provides support for the CIS Forum.

LEADERSHIP SEARCH

Why you should become a CIS Leadership Search Candidate & apply for your next leadership position

The membership community values CIS for its experience and guidance during times of transition. Our global team at CIS have first-hand experience in applying for leadership positions and conducting searches for schools and are thus trusted sources of support and guidance. Working with CIS to apply for a leadership position means that you will have access to our extensive network of international schools (over 475 in 110 countries) that regularly use our services to seek their next school leader. Schools look to CIS to find qualified leadership candidates who share our organisational values. School leaders looking for new and challenging international positions can work with CIS in several ways:

Research & Development Committee

The CIS Research & Development Volunteer Advisory Committee is an ad hoc advisory committee consisting of appointed professionals from CIS School and Higher Education communities with expertise in various disciplines. The Committee serves as a link between member schools and universities on matters relating to research in international education. In a consultative role, the committee supports the development of future CIS services, evaluating and helping to prioritise research and development projects.

Affiliated Consultants

CIS Affiliated Consultant Members are individuals who are part of a global network of schools, higher education institutions, educators and organizations in more than 100 countries dedicated to quality international education. Through this unique membership opportunity, CIS refers Affiliated Consultants to CIS Member Schools seeking dedicated expertise with educational improvement projects.

CIS connects Affiliated Consultant Members with more than 660 Member Schools in more than 100 countries. Consultants have a unique opportunity to reach CIS members through member-only resources and outreach programs throughout the school year.

Criteria

Affiliated Consultant Membership is available to individuals who provide consulting services to primary and secondary schools with international programs. Individuals applying for membership may be working independently or as part of a consulting group or organisation. The number of individuals who are offered an Affiliated Consultant Membership is limited to ensure the number of consultants available matches the demand by CIS Member Schools. Applications are accepted if the individual meets the criteria for membership.

Each individual applying for membership must meet the following criteria:

  • Areas of expertise match identified areas of need
  • At least two years of current, relevant consulting experience with international schools.
  • Positive recommendations from administrators of (at least) two schools where consulting services have been provided and completed
  • A clear criminal record check in the country of residence
  • All members are required to follow the CIS Code of Ethics for Affiliated Consultants

The current priority consulting areas are:

  • Appraisal and professional development
  • Curriculum design and delivery
  • Identification and application of technology
  • Intercultural Competence
  • Leadership training and school governance
  • Strategic planning
  • Student Wellbeing: Protection, Safety, & Security
