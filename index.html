Welcome to International School of Education

International Education Careers

The Council of International Schools has more than 30 years experience helping school educators and leaders find opportunities in high quality schools around the world. Working with ISE provides educators with access to positions in more than 660 schools that offer an international education. Through the ISE Educator Database, an annual recruitment fair and custom leadership searches, educators and staff looking for new international positions can use ISE as a central resource.

ISE Mission

The mission of International Schools Services is to advance the quality of education for children in international schools by providing innovative services and solutions for learning communities and corporations throughout the world. This is accomplished by working with all groups that are involved in the education process.

For Students

ISE provides a way for secondary school students attending ISE Member Schools and other major national schools around the world to connect with ISE Member colleges and universities. Students interested in learning more about international post-secondary educational opportunities complete the online registration form with help from their guidance counsellor. Their information is entered into a ISE registry that ISE Member colleges and universities access when searching for students to contact about enrolment. ISE Higher Education members include more than 480 accredited universities and colleges in 26 countries that are actively seeking students with an international outlook.

Complete the online registration form.

International Student Award

ISE recognises the outstanding work being done by students attending ISE Member Schools through the ISE International Student Award. Award winning students promote global citizenship and the development of international awareness in their communities.

Learn more about past award winners.

School Teaching and Staff Positions

Educators who are looking for positions at ISE Member Schools can apply to become candidates with ISE and set up an online portfolio. Once approved, your portfolio can be accessed by ISE Member Schools looking to hire new educators and staff. In addition, educators may be invited to participate in the annual ISE Educator Recruitment Fair in London which brings leaders from more than 100 schools around the world to one convenient event. Learn more including how to apply to become a ISE candidate or update your existing portfolio.

We help you to grow and adapt in uncertain times

Schools and systems around the world are trying to discover how to educate young people so that they acquire the knowledge, skills, attitudes, values, and capabilities to live as active, productive, responsible, and participative citizens in modern societies. The same applies to those owning, running, and working in these schools and systems. With ever-diminishing half-lives of knowledge and skills, schools cannot limit themselves to the transmission of set content. Instead, they need to develop and promote flexibility, openness to new ideas, ability to adapt, and courage in the face of the unexpected.

School Leadership Positions

Member Schools work with CIS to promote leadership openings and to recruit specific senior leadership positions including Heads of Schools or Divisional Heads. School leaders are invited to take advantage of our free-of-charge recruitment service and establish an active portfolio which can be made available to schools searching for new leaders.

For Consultants & Supporting Organisations

ISE School and Higher Education Members look to ISE to help them identify and establish linkages with organisations and individuals who offer expertise in international education and who can support them in the development and enhancement of their programmes and services.

What we aim to do:

  • To offer a high quality behaviour support network for schools and other services working with vulnerable children and their families.
  • To support an increased understanding of the needs of children made vulnerable by their Social, Emotional and Mental Health difficulties
    • To maintain high levels of inclusion by increasing capacity to manage challenging behaviour through:
    • Developing the understanding of behaviour as communication, and transferring this into practice
    • Understanding the value of developing a culture of reflective practice
    • Developing peer support
    • Increasing confidence in strategies to work with challenging behaviour.
  • To continue to provide evidenced based practice.
