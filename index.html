International Education Careers

The Council of International Schools has more than 30 years experience helping school educators and leaders find opportunities in high quality schools around the world. Working with CIS provides educators with access to positions in more than 660 schools that offer an international education. Through the CIS Educator Database, an annual recruitment fair and custom leadership searches, educators and staff looking for new international positions can use CIS as a central resource.

We elevate and inform educational conversations worldwide

We are an influential voice for and with our member schools, helping them to reflect deeply and sometimes radically about their impact in their local context and in the world at large. As a visible thought leader and key influencer for and with our members on multiple educational stages, we aim not just to improve education, but to transform it, whether through speaking engagements, publications, or participation in think tanks. Through our commitment to influencing, we are building a broader set of norms, institutions, networks, associations, and systems.

CIS Mission

The mission of International Schools Services is to advance the quality of education for children in international schools by providing innovative services and solutions for learning communities and corporations throughout the world. This is accomplished by working with all groups that are involved in the education process.

For Students

CIS provides a way for secondary school students attending CIS Member Schools and other major national schools around the world to connect with CIS Member colleges and universities. Students interested in learning more about international post-secondary educational opportunities complete the online registration form with help from their guidance counsellor. Their information is entered into a CIS registry that CIS Member colleges and universities access when searching for students to contact about enrolment. CIS Higher Education members include more than 480 accredited universities and colleges in 26 countries that are actively seeking students with an international outlook.

Complete the online registration form.

International Student Award

CIS recognises the outstanding work being done by students attending CIS Member Schools through the CIS International Student Award. Award winning students promote global citizenship and the development of international awareness in their communities.

School Leadership Positions

Member Schools work with CIS to promote leadership openings and to recruit specific senior leadership positions including Heads of Schools or Divisional Heads. School leaders are invited to take advantage of our free-of-charge recruitment service and establish an active portfolio which can be made available to schools searching for new leaders.

Why teach Internationally?

What we aim to do:

  • To offer a high quality behaviour support network for schools and other services working with vulnerable children and their families.
  • To support an increased understanding of the needs of children made vulnerable by their Social, Emotional and Mental Health difficulties
    • To maintain high levels of inclusion by increasing capacity to manage challenging behaviour through:
    • Developing the understanding of behaviour as communication, and transferring this into practice
    • Understanding the value of developing a culture of reflective practice
    • Developing peer support
    • Increasing confidence in strategies to work with challenging behaviour.
  • To continue to provide evidenced based practice.
